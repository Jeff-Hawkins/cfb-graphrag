Jim Harbaugh built his coaching tree across two distinct eras — a dominant run at Stanford before transforming Michigan into a national championship program. His tree spans 1,036 coaches across four degrees of separation, including 56 current or former FBS head coaches.
Five coaches who served directly on Harbaugh's staff went on to become FBS head coaches: D.J. Durkin, David Shaw, Jedd Fisch, Sherrone Moore, and Willie Taggart. Each earned their first HC opportunity coming directly off his staff.
The depth 2 branch reflects the breadth of his influence across both programs. David Shaw's branch produced Derek Mason, Mike Bloomgren, Brian Polian, Lance Taylor, and Mike Sanford Jr. Ed Warinner's branch added Mike Locksley, Chuck Martin, Charley Molnar, Stan Drayton, and David Beaty. D.J. Durkin's branch produced Walt Bell.
At depth 2 there are 125 coaches connected to Harbaugh's tree. By depth 4 that number reaches 502.
Harbaugh's pipeline is notable for its loyalty — many of his closest assistants followed him from Stanford to Michigan, creating a tightly connected staff network that compounded across two major programs over nearly two decades.
