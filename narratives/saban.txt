Nick Saban is the most influential coach in the history of college football — and the data backs it up. His coaching tree spans 1,253 coaches across four degrees of separation, including 68 current or former FBS head coaches.
Eight coaches who served directly on Saban's staff went on to become head coaches at the FBS level: Kirby Smart, Billy Napier, Brent Key, Charles Huff, Charles Kelly, Curt Cignetti, Jeremy Pruitt, and Jim McElwain. Each earned their first HC opportunity directly off Saban's staff.
The depth 2 branch tells the real story of compounding influence. Kirby Smart alone produced Dan Lanning (Oregon), Mel Tucker (Michigan State), Sam Pittman (Arkansas), Shane Beamer (South Carolina), Fran Brown (Syracuse), and Dell McGee (Georgia State) as head coaches — all tracing directly back to Saban. Jim McElwain's branch added Geoff Collins. Billy Napier's branch added Jeff Scott and Michael Desormeaux.
At depth 2 there are 180 coaches connected to Saban's tree. By depth 4 that number reaches 609.
Saban didn't just win seven national championships. He built the coaching infrastructure that now runs a significant portion of Power Four football.
