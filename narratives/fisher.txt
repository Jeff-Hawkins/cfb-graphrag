Jimbo Fisher built one of the quieter but more expansive coaching trees in college football. His tree spans 1,041 coaches across four degrees of separation, including 53 current or former FBS head coaches.
Six coaches who served directly on Fisher's staff went on to become FBS head coaches: Charles Kelly, Larry Porter, Mark Stoops, Maurice Linguist, Mike Elko, and Todd Monken. Each earned their first HC opportunity coming directly off his staff.
The depth 2 branch shows the reach of his influence. Mark Stoops produced Neal Brown and Jon Sumrall as head coaches. Todd Monken's branch added Chip Lindsey. Marcus Freeman at Notre Dame traces back through Greg Hudson.
At depth 2 there are 146 coaches connected to Fisher's tree. By depth 4 that number reaches 533.
Fisher's pipeline ran through LSU, Florida State, and Texas A&M across three decades, producing head coaches at programs ranging from Kentucky to Duke to Georgia Tech — a tree built more on sustained staff loyalty than any single dominant era.
