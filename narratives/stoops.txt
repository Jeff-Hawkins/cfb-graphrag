Bob Stoops built one of the most productive coaching trees in Big 12 history during his two-decade run at Oklahoma. His tree spans 1,167 coaches across four degrees of separation, including 66 current or former FBS head coaches.
Six coaches who served directly on Stoops' staff went on to become FBS head coaches: Brent Venables, Jay Norvell, Josh Heupel, Kevin Sumlin, Kevin Wilson, and Lincoln Riley.
The depth 2 branch shows the reach of the Oklahoma pipeline. Kevin Sumlin's branch alone produced Dana Holgorsen, Kliff Kingsbury, Jake Spavital, David Beaty, and Tony Levine. Josh Heupel's branch added Alex Golesh and Jerry Mack. Brent Venables produced Jeff Lebby. Bill Bedenbaugh's branch added Dana Dimel and Sonny Dykes.
At depth 2 there are 160 coaches connected to Stoops' tree. By depth 4 that number reaches 601.
Stoops' pipeline is defined by its offensive coordinator conveyor belt — Riley, Sumlin, and Heupel all ran his offense before becoming successful head coaches in their own right, creating a downstream tree heavily concentrated in offensive-minded programs.
