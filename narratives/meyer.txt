Urban Meyer is one of the most prolific coaching tree generators in college football history. His tree spans 1,382 coaches across four degrees of separation, including 69 current or former FBS head coaches.
Eleven coaches who served directly on Meyer's staff went on to become FBS head coaches: Charlie Strong, Chris Ash, Dan Mullen, Doc Holliday, Luke Fickell, Ryan Day, Scot Loeffler, Stan Drayton, Steve Addazio, Tim Beck, and Tom Herman. Each earned their first HC opportunity coming directly off his staff.
The depth 2 branch shows how fast the influence compounds. Luke Fickell produced Marcus Freeman at Notre Dame. Dan Mullen's branch added Geoff Collins and Mark Hudspeth. Tom Herman produced Major Applewhite at Houston. Charlie Strong's branch added Jeff Traylor and Sherrone Moore. Ed Warinner's branch generated multiple head coaches including Mike Locksley and Chuck Martin.
At depth 2 there are 218 coaches connected to Meyer's tree. By depth 4 that number reaches 616.
What separates Meyer's tree from most is range — his staff pipelines ran through Florida, Utah, and Ohio State across three distinct program cultures, producing head coaches across the full breadth of the Power Four landscape.
